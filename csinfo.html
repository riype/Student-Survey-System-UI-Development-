Department of Computer Science

Welcome to the Department of Computer Science. The department offers BS, MS, and PhD programs in Computer Science.

Faculty in the department have research interests in networking, architecture, parallel and distributed computing, performance evaluation, software engineering, multimedia, graphics and visualization, databases, software engineering, data mining, security, information systems, artificial intelligence, computer vision, and robotics.

Graduate classes are offered in the late afternoon and evening to accommodate the professionally employed students. Financial aid in the form of graduate assistantships may be available for full- time degree-seeking students. Appropriate courses may be transferred, with advisor approval, into the degree program. The Department of Computer Science offers additional degrees and certificates that are of interest to many MS students. Graduate certificates in computer networking, information security and assurance, and software engineering are available. Course work toward the certificates can be used for credit toward the MS in Computer Science. However, the certificates also may be pursued concurrently with any of the graduate degree programs in the Volgenau School of Engineering.

MS programs are offered in:

  • Computer Science
  • Information Security and Assurance
  • Information Systems
  • Software Engineering

The required courses for the above programs are:

  • Computer Science:
    1. CS 583 - Analysis of Algorithms
  • Information Security and Assurance:
    1. ISA 562 - Information Security Theory and Practices
    2. ISA 656 - Network Security
    3. INFS 612 - Principles and Practice of Communication Networks OR CS 555 - Computer Communications and Networking
  • Information Systems:
    1. INFS 612 - Principles and Practices of Communication Networks
    2. ISA 614 - Database Management
    3. INFS 622 - Information System Analysis and Design
    4. ISA 562 - Information Security Theory and Practice
  • Software Engineering:
    1. SWE 619 - Object-Oriented Software Specification and Construction
    2. SWE 621 - Software Modeling and Architecture Design
    3. SWE 622 - Distributed Software Engineering
    4. ISA 637 - Software Testing
